# 场景一：睡前准备

Characters: Sarah and Mark (A couple)

  • Sarah: Mark, could you set the alarm clock for 7 AM? I need to get up early tomorrow.

  • Mark: Sure. I'm just charging my phone on the bedside table. Have you seen my charger?

  • Sarah: I think it's in the top drawer of the dresser. Also, could you turn off the ceiling light? I want to read for a while with my bedside lamp on.

  • Mark: No problem. Don't stay up too late. You need to fall asleep early.

  • Sarah: I won't. I just need to put away the clothes from the laundry basket. Good night!

  • Mark: Good night. Don't let the bedbugs bite!

# 语法讲解 1:

  1. Could you...?

    • 原型：Could you + verb?
    • 讲解：这是非常礼貌地提出请求的句型，比 "Can you...?" 更正式和客气。

  2. I'm just charging my phone.

    • 原型：Subject + am/is/are + present participle (-ing)
    • 讲解：现在进行时，表示说话时正在进行的动作。这里表示马克正在进行的动作。

  3. I need to get up early. / I want to read for a while.

    • 原型：Subject + need/want + to + verb
    • 讲解：need to do 和 want to do 是常见的动词不定式结构，表示“需要做某事”和“想要做某事”。

# 场景二：清晨起床

Characters: Mom and David (Her son)

  • Mom: David, wake up! Your alarm clock has been ringing for five minutes!

  • David: (Yawning) Ugh, I slept terribly last night. I was tossing and turning and couldn't fall asleep until 2 AM.

  • Mom: I heard you snoring. Now, get up and make your bed. Then, get dressed for school.

  • David: Can I hit the snooze button just once? I'm so tired.

  • Mom: No, you'll oversleep. Open the curtains and let in some light. That will help you wake up.

# 语法讲解 2:

  1. Your alarm clock has been ringing...

    • 原型：Subject + has/have + been + present participle (-ing)
    • 讲解：现在完成进行时，强调动作从过去某一时间开始，一直持续到现在，并且可能还在进行。这里强调闹钟一直响个不停的状态。

  2. I was tossing and turning...

    • 原型：Subject + was/were + present participle (-ing)
    • 讲解：过去进行时，表示在过去某个特定时刻或时间段正在发生的动作。这里描述大卫昨晚睡眠不佳时的持续状态。

  3. ...until 2 AM.

    • 讲解：until 是一个介词，表示动作或状态持续到某一时刻为止。"I couldn't fall asleep until 2 AM" 意思是“在凌晨两点之前我一直没睡着”。

# 场景三：整理卧室

Characters: Lisa and Her Friend, Emma

  • Emma: Wow, your room is a mess!

  • Lisa: I know, I need to tidy up. Could you help me fold these blankets and put them away?

  • Emma: Of course. Where should I put them?

  • Lisa: You can put them on the bench at the end of my bed. I'm going to hang up these clothes in the wardrobe.

  • Emma: Okay. What about these throw pillows?

  • Lisa: Just leave them on the bed. I've already cleaned the mirror and dusted the dresser.

  • Emma: Great! It's looking much better now. A clean room helps you relax.

# 语法讲解 3:

  1. Your room is a mess.

    • 讲解：a mess 是一个名词短语，意思是“一团糟”。be a mess 是固定用法，描述混乱的状态。

  2. I need to tidy up. / I'm going to hang up these clothes.

    • need to do: 同上，表示“需要做”。
    • be going to do: 一般将来时的一种形式，表示计划或打算要做的事情。比 "will" 更具计划性。

  3. I've already cleaned the mirror...

    • 原型：Subject + have/has + past participle
    • 讲解：现在完成时，表示过去发生的动作对现在造成了影响或结果。重点是“镜子现在干净了”这个结果。already（已经）是现在完成时的典型标志词。

# 场景四：讨论睡眠问题

Characters: Tom and Dr. Lee

  • Tom: Dr. Lee, I often have nightmares and don't feel rested when I wake up.

  • Dr. Lee: I see. What do you usually do before you go to bed?

  • Tom: I often watch exciting movies on my tablet in bed.

  • Dr. Lee: That might be the problem. The blue light from screens can make it hard to fall asleep. Try reading a book instead. Also, make sure your pillow and mattress are comfortable. A sleep mask and earplugs might also help you sleep deeper.

  • Tom: Thank you. I'll try that. I hope I can have a good dream for once.

# 语法讲解 4:

  1. I often have nightmares...

    • 讲解：一般现在时，用于描述习惯性、经常性的动作或状态。

  2. ...make it hard to fall asleep.

    • 原型：make + it + adjective + to do something
    • 讲解：这是一个非常有用的结构。it 是形式宾语，真正的宾语是后面的不定式 to fall asleep。直译是“使得入睡这件事变得困难”。

  3. Try reading a book instead.

    • 原型：Try + verb-ing
    • 讲解：try doing something 表示“尝试做某事（作为一种方法或实验）”。这里医生是建议一种可以尝试的方法。
